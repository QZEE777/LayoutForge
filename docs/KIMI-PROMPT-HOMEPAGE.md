# Kimi prompt — Homepage hero and bullseye

Paste the block below into Kimi to get copy and design feedback.

---

**Role:** You are a seasoned graphic designer and conversion copywriter. Review this homepage hero concept and suggest improvements.

**Context:** This is the homepage for **manu2print**, a SaaS for indie authors (Amazon KDP & Kindle). The main product is a **Print Ready Check** — users upload an interior PDF and get a pass/fail report against 26 KDP rules (trim, margins, bleed, etc.). The hero should make that one action unmissable.

**Current concept:**
- **Full-bleed hero** that runs from edge to edge and below the fold (most of the viewport).
- A **giant bullseye** (concentric circles) in ivory/arctic white with burnt-orange and navy accents.
- An **arrow graphic** (bow-and-arrow feel) pointing into the center of the bullseye.
- The **centre of the bullseye is the main CTA**: a round button linking to the PDF upload/check page. Literally “hit the target” = “check your PDF.”
- **Headline** (centred, tight, no single-word orphans): e.g. “Hit the target before you upload to KDP.”
- **Subcopy:** Short, benefit-led, centred (e.g. “26 rules. Trim, margins, bleed. Pass or fix in one click.”).
- **Palette:** Ivory or arctic white as main background; burnt orange for the CTA and arrow; navy for text. Brighter, cleaner than the previous dark theme.
- **Typography:** Bebas Neue for headings; body copy centred and tighter (leading-snug, text-balance).

**What I want from you:**
1. **Copy:** One stronger headline and one subcopy line (centred, no orphans, punchy). Optional second line if it adds clarity.
2. **Visual:** Any tweaks to the bullseye + arrow idea (e.g. weight of rings, position of arrow, “target” metaphor) so it feels more striking and on-brand.
3. **CTA:** Exact label for the centre button (e.g. “Check your PDF”, “Upload & check”, “Hit the target”) and, if useful, a tiny supporting line (e.g. “Upload →”).
4. **Psychology:** One or two subtle conversion/subliminal tweaks (e.g. urgency, clarity, reduction of friction) we can bake into the layout or copy.

Keep the tone professional but approachable. Audience: indie authors and small publishers who use Amazon KDP and want to avoid rejections and re-uploads.

---

**End of prompt.** Use the suggestions to refine the hero copy and, if needed, the bullseye/arrow design in code.
