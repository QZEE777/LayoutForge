## Market landscape (categories)

- **General-purpose AI writing tools:** e.g. Jasper, Writesonic. Focus on content generation (copy, marketing, drafts). Not built for KDP/Kindle workflows or print-ready formatting.
- **AI-assisted book / publishing tools:** e.g. BookBud AI, KDP Genius. Vary by focus (content, formatting, listing). Many are broad or multi-platform.
- **Multi-platform formatters / distributors:** Tools that support KDP plus IngramSpark, Draft2Digital, Kobo, etc. One-size-fits-all trim sizes and rules; not Amazon-specialist.
- **manu2print:** KDP + Kindle only. Print-ready PDFs, KDP-friendly limits, cover/spine calculators, keyword/description tools, Kindle EPUB. AI voice and support are knowledge-base–driven (no invented features).

---

## Why a KDP + Kindle–only focus matters

- **Compliance:** KDP and Kindle have specific trim sizes, page limits (e.g. 828 pages), bleed, and file rules. A specialist tool can bake these in instead of offering dozens of platforms with generic options.
- **Clarity:** Authors get one path: manuscript → print-ready + Kindle-ready. No “pick your platform” sprawl.
- **Trust:** AI and support that only answer from a curated knowledge base (product_overview, features, workflows, troubleshooting, limitations, faq, this file) reduce hallucination and wrong advice.
- **Workflow over hype:** manu2print emphasizes format → check → list → calculate, not “AI writes your book.” The AI assists within the product’s real capabilities.

---

## Common competitor gaps (general)

- **Generic AI:** Tools that generate text without being constrained to product facts or KDP/Kindle rules can give wrong formatting or listing advice.
- **Non–KDP-compliant formatting:** Outputs that don’t match KDP trim, bleed, or page limits cause rejections or rework.
- **Overloaded feature sets:** Many platforms and many features in one product can confuse authors who only need Amazon.
- **No single source of truth:** If the AI isn’t limited to a maintained knowledge base, it may invent features or steps.

manu2print addresses these by: (1) KDP + Kindle only, (2) tools that match known KDP/Kindle constraints, (3) KB-only AI and support, (4) workflow-focused feature set.
